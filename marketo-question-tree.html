Q

"How does Marketo handle lead scoring and lifecycle management?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Lead scoring in Marketo uses two dimensions: demographic (attributes like company size, industry, job title) and behavioral (actions like email opens, website visits, content downloads, webinar attendance). Marketo's Predictive Lead Scoring even uses AI to identify which leads are most likely to convert based on historical data. Lifecycle management defines stages — typically MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead), SAL (Sales Accepted Lead), SQL (Sales Qualified Lead), and stages within your CRM pipeline. Smart Campaigns automate the flow: a lead hits MQL when they score above a threshold, sales accepts it (SAL), and when they engage further with sales content, they move to SQL. This keeps marketing and sales aligned on lead quality.

Q

"Can you explain demographic vs. behavioral scoring?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Demographic scoring looks at fit: Is the company the right size? Is the person in the right role? Are they in an industry where you sell? These attributes determine whether someone is a good target. Behavioral scoring tracks engagement: Did they attend a webinar? Download a case study? Visit the pricing page multiple times? Behavioral signals indicate intent and buying momentum. In Marketo, you weight both — a prospect from a $100M company in your target industry gets demographic points for good fit, but then behavioral points matter more because they show active interest. Together, they give you a much richer picture than either one alone.

Q

"How do we define and automate lead lifecycle stages?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Start by defining what each stage means in your business: MQL means a lead meets demographic criteria and shows early interest (attended a webinar, downloaded content). SAL means sales has accepted the lead and assigned it to a rep. SQL means the sales rep has had a qualifying conversation and the deal is in the pipeline. Marketo automates the flow through Smart Campaigns — when a lead scores above the MQL threshold, it automatically moves to MQL. When a sales rep accepts it in Salesforce, it becomes SAL. As the opportunity progresses, it advances to SQL. Revenue Cycle Analytics tracks how long leads spend in each stage, which stages have the highest conversion rates, and which marketing programs are most effective at moving leads forward.

Q

"We're interested in Account-Based Marketing — how does Marketo support ABM?"

Value

Recommended Response

Marketo is built for ABM. Instead of scoring individual leads, you score entire accounts using account-level attributes (company size, industry, revenue growth) and engagement signals. Marketo lets you map buying groups — the set of stakeholders at each target account who influence the purchase decision. You can track which buying group members are engaging with your content, which roles are most engaged, and where there are gaps in engagement. You build personalized campaigns and content programs tailored to each account's priorities and buying stage. Then you measure ABM effectiveness using account-level engagement and revenue metrics, not just lead-level KPIs. This shift from lead-centric to account-centric is a game-changer for complex, multi-stakeholder sales.

Q

"How does buying group management work?"

Technical

Recommended Response

In Marketo, a buying group is a collection of contacts at a target account who play different roles in the buying decision — executive sponsor, technical evaluator, end-user champion, procurement stakeholder, etc. You define the roles relevant to your sales process, then map contacts to those roles as they engage with you. Marketo tracks which roles have engaged with content, how much of the buying group is engaged, and which roles are still missing. You can set engagement thresholds — e.g., "we need all buying group members at executive and technical levels to be engaged before we can move to SQL." This ensures your sales team doesn't pitch until the entire decision-making group has been involved in the conversation.

Q

"How do we measure ABM program effectiveness?"

Value

Recommended Response

ABM metrics are account-centric, not lead-centric. You measure: account engagement (how much of the buying group is actively engaging), deal progression speed (how fast accounts move through your pipeline), win rate lift (do accounts you ran ABM campaigns against close at a higher rate?), and average deal size. Marketo Measure and Revenue Cycle Analytics attribute revenue to each ABM program, showing which account campaigns generate the highest ROI. You can also track engagement by buying group role — which roles are most engaged, which are lagging, and where you need to adjust your content strategy.

Q

"How does Marketo integrate with our Salesforce CRM?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Marketo and Salesforce are deeply integrated via a two-way native sync. Marketo pushes leads and opportunities to Salesforce, while Salesforce pushes account, contact, and opportunity data back to Marketo. In 2025, Adobe completely overhauled the Salesforce sync for better performance, reliability, and flexibility. Now you can control exactly which fields sync, define custom sync rules, and use Marketo Measure to attribute revenue back to marketing touchpoints within Salesforce. When a sales rep accepts an MQL in Salesforce, Marketo can automatically update that lead's status. When an opportunity closes in Salesforce, Marketo tracks it and attributes revenue to the marketing programs that influenced the deal. It's a tight, bidirectional relationship that eliminates data silos between marketing and sales.

Q

"What about the sync — we've heard it can be problematic?"

Objection

Recommended Response

The sync used to have challenges — delayed syncs, field mapping confusion, duplicate record issues. But the 2025 overhaul addressed most of these. The newer sync is faster, more reliable, and includes better error handling. That said, sync quality depends entirely on how you set it up and govern it. The biggest mistake is syncing too many fields or not defining clear rules about when leads should sync. We implement a selective sync approach — only sync the fields sales actually uses, define crisp rules about which leads should push to Salesforce, and implement data quality processes upstream in Marketo to prevent junk data from reaching sales.

Q

"How do marketing and sales teams collaborate within the platform?"

Discovery

Recommended Response

The Marketo-Salesforce link enables real collaboration. Marketing defines lead scoring and lifecycle stages; sales sees those stages in Salesforce and accepts or rejects leads. Marketo tracks sales engagement with leads (opens, clicks, replies) and uses that feedback to refine scoring — so the platform learns from what sales tells it. Sales can use Marketo's Sales Insight or Salesforce integration to see a contact's engagement history and personalize their outreach. If a lead isn't engaging, marketing can trigger a different nurture program. If a lead suddenly goes quiet after sales touches it, marketing can pause the nurture and let sales work it. It's a true feedback loop, not a one-way lead factory.

Q

"What about Dynamic Chat for conversational marketing?"

Value

Recommended Response

Dynamic Chat is Marketo's AI-powered conversational platform that sits on your website. Visitors start a conversation, and Dynamic Chat engages them with qualifying questions, gathers information, schedules meetings, and passes qualified leads to sales — all without human involvement initially.
