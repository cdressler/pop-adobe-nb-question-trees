Q

"What is Adobe Campaign and what makes it different from other email/marketing platforms?"

Discovery

Recommended Response

Adobe Campaign is a unified campaign management platform that goes far beyond email. It orchestrates personalized customer experiences across email, SMS, push notifications, direct mail, and in-app messaging from a single interface. What sets Campaign apart is its visual workflow canvas, which lets non-technical marketers design complex, multi-wave, multi-channel campaigns with conditional logic and real-time decisioning — no coding required. It's also deeply integrated with Adobe Experience Platform, so you're building campaigns directly on top of unified customer profiles and can activate Real-Time CDP segments instantly.

Q

"What channels does Campaign support beyond email?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Campaign is truly multi-channel. You can orchestrate SMS campaigns with delivery tracking, push notifications to mobile apps, direct mail campaigns with postal integration, in-app messaging, and of course email. You can also integrate with other channels via APIs and custom actions. The power is that all these channels are orchestrated from one workflow — so you can build a journey that sends an email, then if it's opened, triggers an SMS, and if the customer doesn't convert, adds them to a direct mail drop. That level of orchestration is what separates Campaign from single-channel email tools.

Q

"How does Adobe Campaign handle cross-channel orchestration?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Campaign's visual workflow canvas is the heart of orchestration. You drag-and-drop elements (audience segments, channel actions, decision nodes, waits, conditions) to build campaigns without code. Campaign v8's advanced query modeler lets you build complex audience logic directly in the workflow, so you're orchestrating with precision.

Q

"How does the visual workflow canvas work?"

Technical

Recommended Response

It's a drag-and-drop interface where you build workflows like a flowchart. You start with a source (audience segment, trigger, list import), then add activities (send email, send SMS, wait, decision fork, audience composition). Each activity has simple configuration — no SQL or code required. Conditions are built visually — "if email opened, then take path A, else take path B." The canvas gives you full visibility into the campaign logic, so stakeholders can understand exactly what the campaign does. Under the hood, Campaign handles all the execution — scheduling, delivery, retry logic, and real-time decisioning across millions of recipients.

Q

"How does Campaign integrate with Real-Time CDP and AEP?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Campaign v8 is built on AEP. You can directly activate Real-Time CDP segments in Campaign workflows — no exports, no delays. When you select an audience source in a Campaign workflow, you can point to any segment defined in Real-Time CDP, and that segment membership evaluates in real time as the campaign runs. This means if a customer's segment status changes mid-campaign (e.g., they convert and move from "prospect" to "customer"), Campaign automatically respects that change. It's seamless, real-time, and eliminates the data latency issues that plague point-solution integrations.

Q

"Can we use RT-CDP segments directly in Campaign?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Q

"How does Campaign handle real-time vs. batch data?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Campaign v8 handles both seamlessly. Real-time data — like a customer's browse behavior or a purchase event — can trigger campaigns instantly via APIs and event streams. Batch data — like a daily audience export or a weekly segment refresh — can be scheduled as part of workflows. Campaign supports scheduled batch activation of audiences at specific intervals. You can also layer triggering on top of batch workflows, so a batch campaign runs daily but within each batch, individual customer journeys are triggered based on real-time events or behavioral conditions.

Objection

Recommended Response

Campaign includes IP reputation management, domain configuration, authentication setup (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), bounce handling, and complaint rate monitoring. The platform automatically suppresses addresses that consistently bounce or complain, and it supports frequency capping so you don't hammer the same person repeatedly. Campaign v8 also integrates with deliverability data from Adobe's wider customer base, so you benefit from industry intelligence about what works and what doesn't. You also get detailed delivery tracking and reporting, so you can monitor inbox placement rates by ISP and adjust tactics accordingly.

Q

"How does IP warming work when migrating to Campaign?"

Technical

Recommended Response

IP warming is a phased approach where you gradually increase email volume from a new IP address to build reputation with ISPs. Typically you start with a small number of your most engaged subscribers (open rates >50%), then incrementally expand to larger segments over 4–6 weeks as the IP's reputation builds. Campaign can automate this using frequency rules and audience segmentation — you define a warming schedule, and Campaign respects it automatically. The key is patience and monitoring during the ramp-up phase, not trying to send your full volume on day one.

Q

"What tools does Campaign provide for deliverability management?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Campaign v8 includes: (1) Suppression lists for opted-out, bounced, or complained addresses; (2) Frequency capping rules to limit sends per recipient; (3) ISP-specific bounce handling that classifies hard bounces vs. soft bounces; (4) Delivery tracking that shows exactly which ISPs received your mail and whether it hit the inbox, spam folder, or bounced; (5) A/B testing for subject lines and content to optimize open rates; and (6) Integration with external list validation services. You also get detailed reporting on open rates, click rates, bounce rates, and complaint rates segmented by ISP, domain, and campaign type.

Competitive

Recommended Response

Both are enterprise marketing automation platforms, but they differ in approach. Salesforce Marketing Cloud is deeply tied to the Salesforce ecosystem — it's designed for teams already committed to Salesforce CRM. Salesforce Marketing Cloud has stronger Salesforce CRM alignment but weaker integration with non-Salesforce data sources. The choice depends on your CRM strategy and broader Adobe/Salesforce ecosystem commitments.
