Q

"What is Journey Optimizer and how is it different from Campaign or our current ESP?"

Discovery

Recommended Response

Adobe Journey Optimizer (AJO) is a purpose-built orchestration platform that designs, executes, and optimizes personalized customer journeys across every channel in real time. Unlike Campaign, which is a batch-focused marketing automation tool, or your current ESP, which typically handles email only, AJO is built on Adobe Experience Platform and orchestrates omnichannel experiences — email, SMS, push notifications, in-app messaging, web, and more — all triggered by real-time customer events and powered by unified customer profiles. Think of it as the difference between scheduling campaigns and orchestrating conversations.

Q

Technical

Recommended Response

Q

"How does the journey canvas work for building customer journeys?"

Technical

Recommended Response

AJO's visual journey canvas is the heart of the platform. You drag and drop journey activities (send email, send SMS, make a decision, wait for an event) onto a canvas and connect them with logic flows. The canvas supports complex conditional logic (if customer is VIP, send premium offer; otherwise, send standard offer), audience eligibility rules, frequency capping, and multi-armed A/B testing, all designed intuitively so your team can iterate fast.

Recommended Response

Q

"How does AJO handle real-time triggered messages vs. batch campaigns?"

Technical

Recommended Response

AJO handles both seamlessly, but they work differently. Real-time triggered journeys listen for an event (customer made a purchase, abandoned cart, visited a specific page) and send an immediate, personalized response within seconds. Batch campaigns send to a scheduled audience segment at a specific time. AJO excels at the triggered side — that's where the conversion uplift happens. Real-time abandoned cart messages have 10x higher ROI than batch promotions. AJO can also run batch-initiated journeys, where everyone in a segment enters a journey at a specific time and then follows personalized paths based on their interactions.

Q

"What about frequency capping and message fatigue?"

Objection

Recommended Response

AJO has sophisticated frequency management built in. You can set rules like "max 3 emails per week," "wait 2 hours between push notifications," or "suppress if customer received more than 5 messages this month across all channels." Frequency caps are evaluated in real time as journeys execute, so a customer who's already received their weekly email limit won't get another one, even if they trigger a new journey. You can also use suppression rules based on engagement (suppress inactive users), business logic (don't message during support tickets), or explicit preference data. This prevents burnout while maximizing delivery for the messages that matter.

Yes, exactly the same. Real-Time CDP builds audience segments in AEP, and AJO reads those same profiles and segments in real time. Segments are defined once in RT-CDP, used everywhere including AJO, Target, Analytics, advertising platforms. Changes to a segment definition automatically propagate to AJO within milliseconds.

Q

"How do AJO and Target work together for web personalization?"

Technical

Recommended Response

AJO and Target complement each other perfectly. AJO orchestrates customer journeys (email, SMS, push sequences) and can include "send a web personalization offer" as a journey step, and that triggers Target on your website. Alternatively, when a customer is on your website and Target shows them a personalization experience, that interaction becomes an event that AJO can listen for and trigger follow-up messaging. So a customer sees a Target offer on your homepage, AJO detects they viewed it, and automatically sends an SMS or email with the complementary offer 2 hours later. Web + messaging working in concert to drive conversion.
