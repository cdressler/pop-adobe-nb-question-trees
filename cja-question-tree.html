Q

"What is CJA and how is it different from Adobe Analytics?"

Discovery

Recommended Response

CJA is Adobe's next-generation analytics platform built on Adobe Experience Platform. While Adobe Analytics is siloed to digital touchpoints (web, mobile, app), CJA unifies online and offline data from every source including websites, mobile apps, call centers, in-store transactions, email, loyalty programs, into a single cross-channel view. CJA connects directly to AEP datasets, so you work with unified customer profiles, apply customer identity to every event, and analyze journeys without artificial session windows. It's analytics designed for a fragmented customer journey.

Q

"How does cross-device identity stitching work in CJA?"

Technical

Recommended Response

CJA relies on AEP's identity graph, which stitches together customer IDs across devices. When a user logs in on mobile and then on desktop, the identity graph recognizes it's the same person. CJA's "stitching" feature reconstructs the journey by replacing anonymous device IDs with the authenticated customer ID retroactively. This means a customer who researched on their phone, added to cart on tablet, and bought on desktop shows up as one journey, not three fragmented visits. The result is accurate cross-device attribution and cohort analysis.

Q

"Can our existing analysts transition easily?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Yes, the interface is familiar, but the data model is different. Adobe Analytics analysts are used to thinking in sessions and visits; CJA works with events and customer IDs. And in CJA, dimensions are properties of customers or events, not site sections, so the mental model shifts slightly. However, analysts with 6–12 months of Adobe Analytics experience typically become productive in CJA within 2–3 weeks of hands-on use. We recommend pairing them with a CJA power user during the transition. The bigger lift is retraining business stakeholders on how to ask cross-channel questions instead of channel-specific ones.

Q

"How does CJA handle attribution and journey analysis?"

Value

Recommended Response

CJA's attribution panel is purpose-built for multi-touch attribution. It supports standard models (first-touch, last-touch, linear, time-decay) and algorithmic/data-driven attribution that uses machine learning to determine the true influence of each touchpoint. For journey analysis, CJA's flow diagrams and fallout visualizations let you see the exact sequence of events across channels, which touchpoints lead to conversion, where do customers drop off. CJA calculates attribution retroactively against any conversion metric, so you can ask "which touchpoints led to this purchase" across web, email, call center, and store visits. This level of granularity is impossible in traditional single-channel analytics tools.

Q

"Can we build custom attribution models?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Yes. The attribution panel includes pre-built models (first, last, linear, time-decay, U-shaped, J-shaped), and you can layer in algorithmic attribution to let the data determine weights. If you need something truly custom, e.g., "give 50% credit to the last brand touchpoint and 50% split among everything before it," CJA's calculated metrics can be configured to that level. You can also build data-driven models by defining your own conversion rules and touchpoint sequences. The flexibility is exceptional compared to tools where attribution is locked into a few preset options.

Q

"How granular can we get with path analysis?"

Technical

Recommended Response

Very granular. CJA's flow and fallout visualizations work at the event level, so you can trace: "customers who viewed product X, then clicked category Y, then spent 30+ seconds on pricing page, then abandoned," across any combination of channels and behavioral signals. Cohort tables let you segment paths by customer attributes (new vs. repeat, geography, loyalty tier). And you can apply filters and conditions to answer almost any path question. The only limit is your data, CJA has no row limits, so you can analyze paths for millions of customers without sampling or aggregation.

Beyond licensing, costs include: (1) integration work to connect your data sources to AEP (typically 4–8 weeks with a partner like POP); (2) analyst upskilling and enablement; (3) ongoing metrics governance and data architecture support. But the reduction in manual analytics work and Tableau/Looker licensing often offsets this within 12 months. And the insights CJA unlocks, optimized attribution, cross-channel behavior understanding, all of which typically drive revenue gains that dwarf the tool cost.
